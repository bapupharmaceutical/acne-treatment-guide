# Understanding and Managing Rosacea

Rosacea is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that primarily affects the face. It is often mistaken for acne but requires different management.

## Common Symptoms

-   **Facial Redness:** Persistent flushing or redness in the center of the face.
-   **Visible Blood Vessels:** Small, spider-like veins (telangiectasia).
-   **Papules and Pustules:** Small red bumps that may resemble acne.
-   **Eye Irritation:** Ocular rosacea can cause dry, itchy eyes.

## Treatment Options

Management focuses on controlling triggers and reducing inflammation.

1.  **Topical Treatments:**
    -   **Ivermectin:** Highly effective for the inflammatory lesions of rosacea. [Ivermectin 1% Cream](https://pillvibe.com/product-detail/ivermectin-1-cream-for-rosacea-treatment) is a standard treatment for subtype 2 rosacea.
    -   **Azelaic Acid:** Excellent for reducing redness and bumps. (See our [Azelaic Acid Guide](azelaic-acid-guide.md)).
2.  **Trigger Avoidance:** Common triggers include spicy foods, alcohol, extreme temperatures, and stress.
3.  **Gentle Skincare:** Avoid harsh scrubs or alcohol-based toners.
