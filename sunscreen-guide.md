# Sunscreen: The Most Important Step in Acne Treatment

When using active ingredients like retinoids or acids, your skin becomes significantly more sensitive to UV radiation. Without proper sun protection, acne scars can darken, and inflammation can worsen.

## Why Sunscreen is Non-Negotiable

1.  **Prevents PIH:** UV exposure darkens Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH).
2.  **Protects the Barrier:** Active treatments like [Tazret Forte](https://pillvibe.com/product-detail/tazret-forte-cream-for-healthy-skin) can thin the stratum corneum temporarily.
3.  **Prevents Aging:** 80% of visible skin aging is caused by the sun.

## Types of Sunscreens

-   **Physical (Mineral):** Uses Zinc Oxide or Titanium Dioxide. Best for sensitive skin.
-   **Chemical:** Uses organic filters to absorb UV rays. Often has a more "elegant" finish for daily wear.

## How to Apply

-   **The Two-Finger Rule:** Apply two full strips of sunscreen to your index and middle fingers for the face and neck.
-   **Reapplication:** Every 2 hours if outdoors, or after sweating/swimming.

## Recommended Routine Integration

Apply sunscreen as the **final step** of your morning routine, after your moisturizer and before any makeup. If you use [Ezanic Cream](https://pillvibe.com/product-detail/ezanic-cream) in the morning, wait for it to dry completely before applying SPF.
