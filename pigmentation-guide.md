# Managing Hyperpigmentation and Dark Spots

Hyperpigmentation occurs when the skin produces excess melanin, leading to dark spots or patches. Common types include Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH), Post-Inflammatory Erythema (PIE), and Melasma.

## Types of Pigmentation

-   **PIH:** Brown or black spots left after an acne lesion heals.
-   **PIE:** Red or pink marks caused by dilated capillaries during the healing process.
-   **Melasma:** Symmetrical patches often caused by hormonal changes and sun exposure.

## Effective Ingredients

1.  **Hydroquinone:** Often considered the gold standard for skin lightening. [Eukroma Cream](https://pillvibe.com/product-detail/eukroma-cream) contains hydroquinone and is used to treat melasma and dark spots.
2.  **Azelaic Acid:** Helps fade PIH by inhibiting tyrosinase. (Reference: [Azelaic Acid Guide](azelaic-acid-guide.md)).
3.  **Retinoids:** Increase cell turnover to bring pigmented cells to the surface faster. (Reference: [Retinoids Guide](retinoids-guide.md)).
4.  **Sunscreen:** Critical for preventing further darkening of spots.

## Treatment Routine

Consistency is key. Results typically take 8-12 weeks to become visible.
